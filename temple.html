About Us

The Organisation

The Temple Dance Studio is a cultural initiative dedicated to reviving and illuminating India’s neglected and lesser-known temples through the transformative power of classical dance. Rooted in tradition and guided by purpose, our vision is to restore the spiritual, cultural, and historical significance of temple heritage that once stood as the foundation of Indian civilization.

We believe dance is not merely a performing art, but a sacred medium of expression — a way to preserve stories, convey devotion, and reconnect communities with their roots. Historically, temples were centers of education, art, philosophy, and social life. They nurtured knowledge systems that shaped generations.

In today’s rapidly evolving and mechanized educational environment, this profound connection between temples, arts, and learning is gradually fading. The Temple Dance Studio strives to bridge that gap by inspiring the younger generation to rediscover and embrace the cultural foundations from which Indian education and artistic traditions were born.

Through performances, training, outreach programs, and cultural initiatives, we aim to breathe new life into our heritage, restore pride in our ancestral legacy, and ensure that the timeless values of temple culture continue to inspire future generations.

About Us

The Founder

Himansee Katragadda

B.Tech, LLB  ·  Founder & Chairman

Himansee is the Founder and Chairman of The Temple Dance Organisation. A distinguished Kuchipudi artiste, educator, and cultural visionary, she is dedicated to preserving and reviving India’s temple heritage through the sacred art of classical dance.

She is a ‘B High’ Grade Artiste of Doordarshan Kendra (2022). She has achieved national distinction having been honored by performing Kuchipudi at the 2011 January Republic Day Parade and at Kartavya Path, New Delhi, in the esteemed presence of the President and Prime Minister of India.

Her artistic journey includes performances at Ganga Mahotsav (2021), IIT Patna (2021), Nilatur Bhagavata Mela (2022), SAM Utsav (2022), Khajuraho Dance Festival (2023), Kalakruti (2023), Balamurali (2023), Thalamur Dance Festival (2023), and Natyanjali Chidambaram (2023). Internationally: Yuva Festival USA (2022), NATA Dallas (2022), TANA Philadelphia (2023), North Carolina USA, AKTA Singapore, Colombo Festival Sri Lanka, Lakshadweep Formation Day (2023), and TCI Festivals in Andaman & Nicobar Islands.

An impassioned artiste of SPIC MACAY, she conducted Kuchipudi workshops for government school students in Vijayawada and Guntur. She served as Visiting Faculty at SRM University, AP, and was the youngest Jury Member for NCERT National Kala Utsav 2023 at YASHADA, Pune, organized by NCERT and the Ministry of Education.

Established in 2018 by the visionary Kuchipudi exponent Himansee Katragadda, the School of Fine Arts is a premier center for classical arts dedicated to the holistic development of dancers, embracing three vital pillars of professional training.

💃

Kuchipudi Dance

Rigorous technical training in Nritta, Nritya, and Natya — the three dimensions of classical Indian dance.

🎵

Carnatic Music

Understanding Rhythm (Tala) and Melody (Raga) to enhance expressive performance and musicality.

🤼

Yoga

Building the core strength, balance, and mental control required for high-stamina recitals and stage presence.
